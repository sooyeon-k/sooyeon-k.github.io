Social Good Data Science Consultant

As a Data Science Consultant for Data Science Society at UC Berkeley, I worked on a team of 5 students for The Nature Conservancy where we developed predictive models using advanced numerical techniques and machine learning to support water conservation strategies in California. I implemented dynamic windowing methods to improve the accuracy of these models, utilizing performance metrics such as KGE and RÂ². Additionally, I built an interactive Streamlit dashboard for real-time data visualization, enabling stakeholders to interact with and make decisions based on up-to-date conservation data.

Project Presentations

Final Deliverable

Our final deliverable summarizes predictive models we developed to forecast water runoff using weather and streamflow data. We applied dynamic windowing and machine learning techniques to improve accuracy, and visualized results in a custom dashboard to support conservation decision-making by The Nature Conservancy.



Midterm Deliverable

The midterm report outlines our exploratory data analysis, feature selection process, and baseline modeling results. We identified trends in regional water usage and discussed early modeling performance, setting the stage for refining prediction accuracy in the final phase.
