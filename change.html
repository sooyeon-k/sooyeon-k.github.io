Social Good Data Science Consultant

As a Data Science Consultant for the Data Science Society at UC Berkeley, I worked on a team of 6 students for Change.org where we analyzed over 3 million petitions using NLP tools to extract strategic insights from a five-year petition dataset. I developed a “quality petition” metric and built several regression models—linear, Poisson, and negative binomial—achieving 86% accuracy in predicting petition success. I also implemented Google’s Perspective API to identify harmful or spam petitions and support the platform’s moderation system.

Final Deliverable

This presentation summarizes our key findings, including feature importance from petition metadata, modeling approaches to predict success, and recommendations for identifying harmful content. It also demonstrates how Change.org can use text-based analytics to improve petition discoverability and platform health.
